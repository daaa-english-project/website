Impact of Globalization on the Environment

Introduction

Our current production model is based on globalization, which relies on extensive trade between countries and outsourcing. While it has brought some benefits, it has significant environmental and societal impacts.

Environmental Impacts of Globalization

Negative Impacts Positive Impacts
Greenhouse Gas Emissions: Global goods transport and massive industrialization contribute significantly to CO2 emissions, exacerbating climate change. Clean Technology Transfer: Globalization facilitates the sharing of environmental technologies with developing countries.
Overexploitation of Natural Resources: Increasing demand for raw materials leads to deforestation, soil depletion, and freshwater scarcity. Increased Awareness: Greater awareness of environmental issues encourages sustainable practices.
Pollution: Intensified industrial and agricultural activities pollute air, water, and soil, harming biodiversity and human health. International Cooperation: Globalization supports international agreements to combat climate change and protect biodiversity.
Biodiversity Loss: Natural habitats are destroyed for intensive agriculture, urbanization, and global trade infrastructure. Global Awareness: The interconnectedness brought by globalization helps amplify the urgency of tackling environmental challenges worldwide.

Water Crisis

The water crisis is one of the greatest threats to our planet. It stems from a combination of factors such as climate change, population growth, industrialization, and poor water resource management.

  • Main Causes: Population growth, climate change, industrial water pollution, and overconsumption.
  • Consequences: Scarcity of drinking water, food insecurity, conflicts, and biodiversity loss.

Impact on Biodiversity

Globalization leads to pollution, which in turn reduces biodiversity. The introduction of non-native species can have irreversible effects on local ecosystems.

  • Example: American crayfish in Europe were introduced for fishing but proliferated, destroying habitats and outcompeting native species.
  • Deforestation: Large-scale deforestation, particularly in the Amazon, is a direct consequence of global demand for resources.

Maritime Transport

Maritime transport plays a crucial role in globalization, handling about 90% of global trade by volume. A significant portion of ships transport energy products like oil and gas.

  • Energy Cargo Ships: Around 30% of the world's ships are dedicated to transporting energy products.
  • CO2 Emissions: Shipping contributes about 2.5% of global CO2 emissions.
